Symptoms of Sepsis
Common symptoms of sepsis include fever or low body temperature, chills,
rapid heart rate, rapid breathing, confusion or disorientation,
extreme pain or discomfort, low blood pressure, and reduced urine output.
Symptoms can worsen quickly and require urgent medical attention.

Causes of Sepsis
Sepsis is caused by the body’s extreme response to infection.
Common causes include pneumonia, urinary tract infections,
abdominal infections, bloodstream infections, and infected wounds.

Risk Factors for Sepsis
People at higher risk include older adults, infants, people with weakened
immune systems, and those with chronic illnesses such as diabetes,
cancer, kidney disease, or liver disease.

Diagnosis of Sepsis
Sepsis is diagnosed using clinical signs, vital signs, blood tests,
and identification of an infection source.
Early diagnosis improves survival.

Treatment of Sepsis
Treatment includes immediate antibiotics, intravenous fluids,
oxygen therapy, and close monitoring.
Severe cases may require intensive care or surgery.

Prevention of Sepsis
Sepsis can be prevented by practicing good hygiene, treating infections
early, keeping wounds clean, getting recommended vaccinations,
and managing chronic health conditions.

When to Seek Medical Help
Sepsis is a medical emergency.
Seek immediate medical care if infection symptoms are accompanied by
confusion, difficulty breathing, or very low blood pressure.
